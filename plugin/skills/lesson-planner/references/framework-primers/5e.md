# 5E — Engage, Explore, Explain, Elaborate, Evaluate

BSCS-originated constructivist model, widely adopted in science and extensible to any content area.

## Core phases

1. **Engage** — hook students; surface prior knowledge and misconceptions. (5-10 min)
2. **Explore** — hands-on investigation or problem-solving BEFORE the teacher explains the concept. Students grapple with phenomena. (15-25 min)
3. **Explain** — teacher formalizes the concept using student-generated observations as the anchor; introduces vocabulary. (10-15 min)
4. **Elaborate** — students apply the concept to a new context, problem, or deeper task. (15-30 min)
5. **Evaluate** — formative or summative check for mastery, interwoven throughout or at the end. (5-10 min)

A single lesson can touch all 5 phases; a unit may spend one full period per phase.

## How it shapes a lesson plan

- Explore precedes Explain — the model is strictly inverted from "lecture then lab"
- Each phase names an observable student behavior, not a teacher behavior
- Evaluate appears in every lesson, even if informal

## Language signals

- "Students observe… before being told…"
- "The teacher introduces the term [X] AFTER students have encountered the phenomenon"
- "Application to a new context"
- Phase labels in the plan itself

## Common pitfalls

- Letting "Explain" slide back into opening lecture
- Skipping "Elaborate" for time; treating a warmup as "Engage" with no real curiosity hook
- Stretching one phase across multiple days without tracking the arc
