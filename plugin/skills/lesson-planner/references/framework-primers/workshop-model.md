# Workshop Model

Mini-lesson + independent/small-group practice + share. Originated in Lucy Calkins' reading/writing workshop work; now widespread in ELA and math.

## Core structure

1. **Mini-lesson** (8-15 min) — teacher explicitly teaches ONE strategy; ends with a clear charge ("today, try…")
2. **Workshop time** (25-40 min) — students work independently or in small groups applying the day's strategy. Teacher confers with individuals or pulls small groups.
3. **Mid-workshop teach** (2-3 min, optional) — brief whole-class pivot if a pattern emerges in conferring.
4. **Share** (5-10 min) — one to three students share; teacher names what they tried; class reflects on the strategy.

## How it shapes a lesson plan

- The mini-lesson is **one strategy**, not a full content dump
- Workshop time is protected; teacher is not the center of attention
- Conferring notes are the primary formative assessment
- Every day ends with a share that reinforces the strategy

## Language signals

- "Today's strategy: …"
- "Confer with 4-5 readers/writers; take conferring notes"
- "Share: ___ will share how they used ___"
- "Anchor chart: …"

## Common pitfalls

- Mini-lessons that run 25+ minutes and swallow workshop time
- Workshop time that becomes silent worksheet completion
- Skipping the share on busy days
