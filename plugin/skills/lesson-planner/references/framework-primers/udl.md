# UDL — Universal Design for Learning

CAST's framework for designing accessible instruction from the start rather than retrofitting accommodations.

## Core principles (provide multiple means of…)

1. **Engagement** — the "why" of learning. Offer choice, relevance, autonomy, community.
2. **Representation** — the "what". Present content in multiple formats (text, audio, visual, hands-on).
3. **Action & Expression** — the "how". Let students demonstrate mastery in multiple ways.

Each principle has three guidelines (9 total in the CAST 2.2 framework), each with checkpoints. The skill does not need to cite specific checkpoint numbers unless the teacher asks.

## How it shapes a lesson plan

- Every lesson offers at least TWO options somewhere (in content, process, or product)
- Explicit supports exist for students with differing processing needs (text-to-speech, visual supports, sentence frames)
- Assessment is flexible — students can show what they know in more than one way

## Language signals

- "Students may choose to…"
- "Options include…"
- "Provide the text in large print, audio, and digital form."
- "Students demonstrate mastery via [written / oral / visual / performance]."

## Common pitfalls

- Treating UDL as a checklist of accommodations instead of a design principle
- Offering "choice" between two trivially-different options
- Claiming UDL while delivering a single-format lecture
