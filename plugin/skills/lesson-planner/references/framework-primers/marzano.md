# Marzano — High-Yield Instructional Strategies

Robert Marzano's nine categories of instructional strategies identified as having the largest effect on student achievement (meta-analysis of ~100 studies).

## The nine categories

1. **Identifying similarities and differences** — compare, contrast, classify, analogies/metaphors
2. **Summarizing and note-taking**
3. **Reinforcing effort and providing recognition**
4. **Homework and practice**
5. **Nonlinguistic representations** — graphic organizers, images, physical models, kinesthetic
6. **Cooperative learning**
7. **Setting objectives and providing feedback**
8. **Generating and testing hypotheses**
9. **Cues, questions, and advance organizers**

## How it shapes a lesson plan

- Strategy selection is intentional: every activity tied to one of the nine
- Objectives are stated and feedback is specific (not just "good job")
- Graphic organizers appear regularly — nonlinguistic representation is chronically underused
- Cooperative structures are named (Think-Pair-Share, jigsaw, numbered heads, etc.), not left vague

## Language signals

- "Compare/contrast: Venn diagram of ___ vs. ___"
- "Students generate hypotheses about ___"
- "Advance organizer: ___"
- "Cooperative structure: [named structure]"

## Common pitfalls

- Treating the nine as a menu to check off
- Calling "group work" cooperative learning without assigned roles or interdependence
- "Feedback" = a letter grade at the end
