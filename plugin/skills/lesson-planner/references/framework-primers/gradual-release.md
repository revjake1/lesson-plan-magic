# Gradual Release of Responsibility — I Do / We Do / You Do

Fisher & Frey model. Shifts cognitive load from teacher to student within a lesson.

## Core phases

1. **Focus lesson / I Do** — teacher models the skill aloud; thinks out loud; names what they're doing. (~10 min)
2. **Guided instruction / We Do** — teacher and students work a parallel problem together; teacher scaffolds, removes scaffolds as students pick up. (~15 min)
3. **Collaborative / You Do Together** — students work in pairs or small groups on a near-peer task; teacher confers. (~15 min)
4. **Independent / You Do** — students apply the skill solo; teacher monitors. (~10 min)

Order is not always linear — effective teachers cycle back to We Do when students struggle. The skill plan should name each phase by role, not just time.

## How it shapes a lesson plan

- Every major skill gets all four phases somewhere in the arc (may span multiple days)
- "Workbook time" is not "You Do" unless students are applying the taught skill independently
- Teacher plans what to model BEFORE what to practice

## Language signals

- "Model: teacher thinks aloud through [procedure]."
- "Pairs practice [task]; teacher confers."
- "Independent application: students complete [X] alone."
- Explicit phase tags (I Do / We Do / You Do) in the plan

## Common pitfalls

- Jumping to "You Do" before students can articulate what the teacher modeled
- Calling a worked example "I Do" without thinking aloud
- Turning "We Do" into a whole-class Q&A where one student answers and the rest watch
